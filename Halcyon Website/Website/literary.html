Literary Events

1. JAM (Open Event)
PRELIMS:
He/ She would be objected for following reasons: (based on the following rounds)
• Usage of slang, direct speech and abbreviations.
• Standard Formats are not to be used.
• Plagiarism or Redundancy.
• Deviation from the topic/time wasting tactics.
• Unnecessary stress on a word or letter.
• Obsession with words.
• Incoherence.
• Dramatization.
• JAM master’s judgment is final and binding.

FINALS:
He/ She would be objected for following reasons: (based on the rounds)
• Usage of slang, direct speech and abbreviations.
• Usage of standard formats.
• Plagiarism or Redundancy.
• Deviation from the topic/time wasting tactics.
• Unnecessary stress on a word/letter.
• Obsession with words.
• Incoherence.
• Dramatization.
• JAM master’s judgment is final and binding.
2. 20Q (Open Event)
PRELIMS:
• The team members will be separated in 2 different rooms and will be given a list of 20 items and a blank sheet of paper.
• The participants will have to write 3 clues for each of the items in their sheet and these clues shall be single word and proper nouns are NOT allowed. Time allotted for this is 20 min.
• After this, sheets are exchanged with their respective team mates, who then will have 10 minutes to guess the answers based on the clues given.
• They are allowed 3 guesses for every question.
• Violation of the above rules will lead to disqualification.
• Decision of the JUDGES is final and binding.

FINALS:
• Both the members of the team play this round together.
• Participants have to choose a category and a topic is given to them at random from the chosen category.
• One member of the team knows the topic while the other tries to guess it by asking a maximum of 20 questions.
• None of the questions can be asked directed towards any name or any part of the topic specifically.
• The time limit for asking questions and guessing the topic is 4 minute.
• Violation of the above rules will lead to disqualification.
• Decision of the JUDGES is final and binding.
3. Quiz (Open Event)
• 2 members per team.
• Number of rounds will be decided by the quizmaster.
• One written preliminary test, from which top teams will be selected for the finals.
• Quizmaster’s decision will be final and binding.
4. Turn Coat (Open Event)
• Individual event.
• Topic will be given on the spot and 3 minutes preparation time will be given.
• Time limit: 3 minutes.
• The same participant speaks FOR and AGAINST the topic.
• Decision of the JUDGES is final and binding.
5. Three Monkeys
• 3 teams per college with 3 members each.
• Time allotted to this event will be (5 + 1) min.
• Person A will be provided with a poster which he/she has to describe verbally to Person B, B will have to enact the description to Person C who in turn has to represent it in a pictorial form.
• Once the participants are finished with their tasks, they should not interfere again.
• The top teams will qualify for the finals depending upon the time constraints and no. of participants.
• Decision made by the JUDGES is FINAL.
6. Debate
• 3 teams per college with 2 members each.
• The topic for the debate will be announced 24 hours prior to the event.
• One participant speaks for the topic and one against.
• Each participant is given max time of 5 min.
• Judging will be based on the overall team performance.
• Usage of abusive language or comments made against any caste, creed, sex or religion is grounded for disqualification.
• The participants will be judged on various aspects such as depth of speech, relevance to the topic and knowledge of the topic.
• Decision of the JUDGES is final and binding.
7. Dumb Charades
• 3 teams per college.
• 3 members per team.
• Each member of the team needs to enact at least once in a round.
• General DC rules apply, for e.g.: no lettering, no phonetics (Phonetics can be used in certain rounds only) etc.
• The top teams will qualify for the finals depending upon the time constraints and no. of participants.(Specifications of various rounds will be announced at the time of the event).
• The decision of the DC master is final.
8. Spell Bee (Open Event)
• 1st round: written elimination round.
• 2nd round: verbal round.
• The meanings of various words will be given of demand.
• Decision of the judges is final and binding.
9. Creative Writing (Open Event)
• Time limit: 75 min.
• Word limit: 1000 words.
• Subject: Topic will be given on the spot.
• Decision of the judges will be final.
10. Blind Fold Texting (Open Event)
• Text of 160 characters will be read out to the blind folded participants, who have to type it in their own phones.
• Participants with most accurate text wins.
• T9 or predictive text is allowed.
• No restriction on phone used.
