Gaming Events

1. Counter Strike
• A team of 5 is allowed.
• The teams must confirm their entries with the game coordinators at the venue 10 min prior to the event.
• Use of obscene language imbibed into the team’s play shall incur disqualification.
• The maps selected for the competition are de_dust2, de_inferno, de_nuke, de_train.
• All rules are subjected to change at the discretion of the gaming coordinators.
• The gaming coordinators decision is final.
• Accessories are allowed at the owner’s risk.
• Teams will be allowed only with valid engineering college ID cards.
• Discrepancies in the formation of the team shall lead to disqualification.
• Official ESL ONE Gaming Rules will be followed, except for number of rounds allotted.
• Cross college team NOT allowed.
2. Anti Chess (Open Event)
• Castling is not allowed.
• No checkmate.
• Each piece has some point.
• Time limit: 5 Minutes.
• 3 wrong moves lead to elimination.
3. FIFA (Open Event)
• Each half of 4 min.
• Joysticks and gamepads are allowed (with REQUIRED software).
• All rules are subjected to change at the discretion of the gaming coordinators.
• Accessories are allowed at the owner’s risk.
• Individual event (knock out).
• The gaming coordinators decision will be final.
4. NFS (Open event)
• 4 participants compete with each other in circuit.
• Winner proceeds to the next round.
• The participants must confirm their entries with the game coordinators at the venue 10mins prior to the event.
• Use of obscene language shall incur disqualification.
• The gaming coordinator’s decision is final.
• Accessories are allowed at the owner’s risk.
• Participants will be allowed only with valid ID cards.
• All rules are subjected to change at the discretion of gaming coordinators.
